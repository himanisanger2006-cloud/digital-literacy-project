Stay Safe Online – Checklist 
General Safety Guidelines 
* Use strong and unique passwords for each account, including a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters.  
* Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on important platforms like email, banking, and social media.  
* Avoid opening suspicious links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.  
* Always verify website URLs—ensure correct spelling and look for “https://” before entering sensitive data.  
* Do not access important accounts using public Wi-Fi networks.  
* Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.  
* Regularly back up important data to prevent loss in case of cyberattacks.  
* Log out of accounts after using shared or public devices.  
 
Financial / UPI Safety Tips 
* Never share your bank details, UPI PIN, or OTP with anyone.  
* Do not approve unknown “Collect Requests” on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.  
* Double-check the recipient’s details before transferring money.  
* Avoid scanning random QR codes from unknown or untrusted sources.  
* Set transaction limits and enable alerts for all financial activities.  
* Immediately report any suspicious transaction to your bank.  
 
Social Media & Awareness 
* Avoid sharing sensitive personal details such as phone number, address, or academic information publicly.  
* Keep your social media profiles private and review privacy settings regularly.  
* Be cautious about friend requests from unknown people.  
* Think carefully before posting—your online presence can affect your future career.  
* Do not click on attractive offers, giveaways, or fake job links shared online.  
* Stay informed about common cyber threats like phishing and scams.  
 
Reporting Cybercrime 
* Report cybercrime incidents on the National Cyber Crime Portal.  
* In case of financial fraud, immediately call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930.  
* Inform your bank and block your account if any suspicious activity is detected.  
* Keep records of messages, emails, or transaction details as evidence for reporting.
